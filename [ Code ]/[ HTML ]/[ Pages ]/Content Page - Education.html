Science Communication MSc (September 2022- November 2023)

After entering the final year of my Bachelors I decided to continue my studies and specialise into science communication, this passion originated in both my educational background with both presentational and video assignments in psychology, as well as my experiences within writing science for the psychology newsletter and further afield experiences such as volunteering within a museum gave me an appreciation for sharing and discussing knowledge with visitors.

Throughout my masters, I was set assignments varying in the different fields of science communication and I really challenged myself to explore as many areas of science within each of these as possible. I approached feature articles discussing artificial intelligence and religion, explored mental illness alongside an OCD specialist for a radio package, presented and narrated a documentary discovering how beavers are thriving on the River Avon and helped developed a digital public initiative to get people talking about climate change (The bus stop science project).

I explored various mediums for science communication during my time on the course. Writing science consisted not only of journalism features and articles, but opinion pieces and press releases too. In terms of science media not only did we experience recording live radio at the BBC Clifton Studios but also prerecording, interviewing for radio, and creating and editing radio packages; as well as filming for documentaries, interviewing specialists on camera, and video editing.

This course also explored the theory behind science communication, including discussing audiences and publics, legal issues within the industry, risk communication, diversity and cultural impacts within science communication. Contemplating the places of scientists within public issues, cultural historical objects within museums in different countries, and how to manage mis- and disinformation in science communication.

This degree concluded with the formation of a dissertation, I approached this qualitatively surrounding the topic of Certainty of Knowledge, a subcategory of personal epistemology running along a spectrum of certainty to uncertainty, where certainty demonstrates a belief that knowledge is unchanging, and uncertainty shows a belief that knowledge is liable to change. I utilised media analysis to investigate how science communicators on YouTube were discussing scientific knowledge and found various techniques which demonstrated both certainty and uncertainty.

Psychology Bsc (Hons) (September 2018 - July 2022)

Achieving a second class upper division in this course was the result of a deep exploration into the vast disciplines within psychology, ranging from neurological and genetic ideas from biological and evolutionary psychology, to ties with group dynamics and environmental influences in social and behavioural psychology. Within my final year there are optional units and I decided to specialise with lesser known psychological topics within gaming, sexuality and cyberpsychology.

During this course I not only wrote various academic essays, but completed various presentations such as one discussing concepts surrounding Hebbian theory, created a video about the importance of defining sex, and displayed a poster supplementing the dissertation I completed in my final year. The project of which my dissertation pertains discussed the connection between characters in Roleplaying Games, and the players that designed them to find consistencies particularly within the characters backstory.

Applied Science (Forensic Science) Level 3 Extended Diploma (September 2016 - July 2018)

11 GCSEs ranging from A* to C This course gave me a broad overview of different areas of science, focused broadly on chemistry, biology and physics in the first year as well as lab safety, the second year brought about specialisation into forensic science with forensic variations of biology, chemistry, entomology, psychology and criminology to name a few.
